Hello!

Sir Tim Berners-Lee invented the World Wide Web back in 1989. Sir Tim is the Director of the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C), a Web standards organization founded in 1994. It develops interoperable technologies (specifications, uidelines, software, and tools) to leas the Web to its full potential. He is a Director of the World Wide Web Foundation which was launched in 2009 to coordinate efforts to further the potential of the Web to benefit humanity.

In 2001 he became a Fellow of the Royal Society. He has been a recipent of several international awards including the Japan Prize, The Prince of Asturias Foundation Prize, The Millenium Technology Prize, and Germany's Die Quadriga Award. In 2004 he was knighted by H.M. Queen Elizabeth, and in 2007 he was awarded the Order of Merit. And lastly, in 2009, he was elected a foreign associate of the National Academy of Sciences.
